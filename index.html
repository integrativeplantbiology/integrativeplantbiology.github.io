About

Profile

I am a plant scientist with research interests spanning crop genomics, transcriptomics, stress biology, comparative genomics, and molecular resources for breeding. My work focuses on understanding how plants respond to environmental and biotic challenges, with applications in crop improvement and sustainable agriculture.

My research includes studies on durum wheat, pea, artichoke, Brassica, and other crop systems, with particular attention to nitrogen stress, abiotic stress responses, chloroplast genomics, plant defense, and genomic tools for trait dissection.

Quick Facts

  • Field: Plant molecular biology & genomics
  • Topics: Transcriptomics, breeding, stress responses
  • Systems: Wheat, pea, artichoke, Brassica
  • Methods: RNA-seq, GBS, comparative genomics, gene networks

Research Interests

Main Research Areas

Crop Genomics & Diversity

Study of genetic diversity, domestication, population structure, and genomic signatures relevant to crop evolution and breeding.

Transcriptomics & Gene Regulation

Analysis of transcriptomic responses under abiotic and biotic stress, with focus on regulatory pathways, transcription factors, and stress adaptation.

Stress Biology

Investigation of plant responses to nitrogen limitation, drought, salinity, parasitic weeds, and other environmental challenges affecting crop performance.

Breeding Resources

Development of genomic resources, candidate genes, and markers to support selection for resilience, productivity, and agronomic improvement.
